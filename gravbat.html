Gravity Battery Model

Freshman Projects: Groups of 5 freshman engineers were tasked with creating a project to inform the public on a current sustainability issue for the Mission Zero Project Expo

We tackled energy intermittency: What happens when there is no wind for turbines to generate energy, or at night when solar panels can't generate energy? Gravity batteries use excess energy generated from renewable sources during times of high production to raise a large mass storing potential energy. When other renewable sources are unable to meet power demands, the weight is released allowing the kinetic energy of the falling mass to power electrical grids.

As Project Manager, I organized weekly meetings ensuring that we stayed ahead of deliverable deadlines and fostered efficient and healthy communication lines between group members crucial to combining separate mechanical and electrical systems into one cohesive mechanism. Once I had our team organized, I filled gaps in work to facilitate a smooth work process. I was in the wood shop with our mechanical lead helping manufacture the frame. I wrote and revised sections of our presentations, Critical Design Review, and Project Report to make sure our deliverables and presentations were direct and professional.

This project taught me how to draw the best out of a collection of individuals. I learned the importance of clear communication between group members, and how to effectively facilitate that communication. I learned how to set deadlines and ensure they are met. Lastly I discovered that by predicting hurdles and bottlenecks in the design process, I could provide assistance where it is needed most to keep maintain forward progress.
