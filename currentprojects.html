2026 Ride Engineering Competition

I am leading the mechanical design of a Lifting Scrambler Ride for the 2026 Ride Engineering Competition (REC). Designing an ASTM F2291 compliant ride system with three independent motion systems has been an engaging challenge. I led the design of a chain drive for central shaft rotation, a direct drive cart rotation system, and a lead screw driven scissor lift to raise and lower the full ride. To justify our design, I have performed calculations for factors of safety for structural failure and bending, as well as sizing motors for torque requirements in accordance with ASTM F2291.

With a successful final design review for a panel industry professionals and CU Boulder Professors in the books, I look forward to receiving materials, building our ride, and writing Factory and Site Acceptance Testing documentation.

Vitruvian Man Puzzle Box – Escape Room

Despite being Project Manager of CU Boulder Theme Park Engineering Design Club's first Escape Room, I didn't want to be left out of puzzle design! I drew on my passion for creating electromechanical systems for interactive technology to design a Vitruvian Man puzzle box. Buttons, levers, and knobs on the sides of the box are used to release solenoid "locks" within the box. By solving all sides of the box, the center column can be twisted and pulled through an internal maze to release the key from the secret internal compartment. I taught myself circuit design and Arduino programing to build a circuit utilizing flyback diodes and transistors to control each of the 4 internal solenoids. With the internal circuit complete, I look forward to combining the electronic and mechanical systems to make an immersive puzzle for our escape room guests.

WALL·E Animatronic

I want to gain experience designing the mechanical systems behind animatronics. WALL·E was the perfect candidate to tackle. To deliver his iconic charm, I did torque calculations to select motors capable of the ranges of motion I desired. I also want to ensure that the electronics are hidden within his character shell to make him look movie realistic. With his head's pan and tilt designed and prototyped, I am looking forward to building out the SolidWorks model to add servo driven arms and motor driven treads.
