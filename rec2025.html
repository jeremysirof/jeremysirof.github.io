Chrono Clash

2025 Ride Engineering Competition: Design and fabricate a motor-driven flat ride for Starburst riders. The ride must adhere to ASTM F2291-24 standards and have proper Factory and Site Acceptance Testing documentation.

I focused on designing and fabricating the ride’s frame around three key constraints: portability, budget, and the ASTM F2291-24 guidelines.

To transport the ride, it had to break down into carry-on and checked luggage. Using wood 2x4s, steel gussets, and ½” bolts, I designed a frame that disassembled and reassembled in under 15 minutes. This made our ride extremely serviceable. When a screw loosened in our gearbox 3 hours into runtime, and 10 minutes before judging, we were able to disassemble, fix the screw, and reassemble our ride, having it functional for the judging period.

Although we initially planned to use metal, I found wood 2x4s to be cheaper, easier to work with, and more than strong enough providing a Factor Of Safety over 200. Using ½” bolts already on hand, we minimized the frame's cost to save budget for more expensive components in other areas of the project. Adhering to ASTM F2291-24 guidelines, the 2x4s, steel gussets, and ½” bolts provided strength magnitudes greater than required. When the judges commented that they thought they could kick the frame without it moving, I laughed and told them, “We have kicked the frame. It doesn’t move.”
