Climbing Cam

CAD and Fabrication: Recreate an object in SolidWorks utilizing engineering drawings and hand measurements

As an avid climber, I jumped at the chance to recreate in SolidWorks the most impressively engineered piece of climbing gear. Cams wedge inside cracks in the rock. Pull the lever, and lobes retract to slot into a crack. Upon release, the lobes partially expand matching the rock's geometry. When a climber slips, the rope pulls the cam applying outward force on the rock, further locking the cam in the rock to prevent dangerous falls.

In my process of recreating the Black Diamond Camelot C4 Cam, I learned how to design organic geometry. The lobes do not use standard shapes making them difficult to design in a software like SolidWorks. By measuring from a reference point on the outer edge and referencing engineering drawings, I was able to reconstruct the smooth curves to closely match the physical object. After completing the individual parts, I created a dynamic assembly that allowed realistic retraction of the lobes controlled by the trigger. I learned how to make clear exploded assembly and how to make readable engineering drawings such that another person could recreate my assembly or manufacture parts to my exact dimensions.
